# Mock Draft

Its draft week in the Steel City! I strongly considered swinging up this weekend, but ultimately I decided I don't like crowds. If the Steelers ever have a top 5 pick, I'd consider travelling for the draft, but while we toil with the worst possible draft position for teams without playoff wins, I won't make the effort.

We've been curating our NFL first round Mock Drafts for over 5 years now (first in 2021). Lets take a look at how we've done with our predictions. Our mock drafts are scored as follows: 2 points for correctly guessing the player a team drafts and 0.5 points for guessing the correct position.

## Group Performance

As a group, we average just under 12 player to team combinations which is pretty solid. That's strongly inflated by our reads of the first 10 picks where we average 7 correct picks. That's much higher than I would have expected, but with about four to seven people making predictions every year maybe the match checks out. Additionally, the media coverage on teams' targets in the first few picks is subject to less smoke screens and misinformation as those teams probably don't care about getting snaked since it would take a king's ransom to trade into the top 10.

## Guessing Prowess

Jeff, both Codys, and me are the only ones with >3 years worth of predictions. Across the past five years, we all have similar scores and are at the mercy of the predictability of the draft

## Talent Scouting

The success of a first round NFL draft pick is not a binary positive or negative outcome. You can count the number of Pro Bowls a player attends, but that's quickly becoming a joke accolade. All-Pros could be useful, but plenty of contributing players aren't selected to an All-Pro team. Signing a second contract with the team that drafted you is a commonly used metric to measure pick success. Its not without its exceptions (eg Rashod Bateman) or ommissions (eg Micah Parsons), but that's the metric I decided to look at. Importantly, we were predicting what players teams will draft and not should draft so this metric is slightly different than what we were going for.

After constructing the previous paragraph, I then checked and saw 13 of the 32 first round picks from 2021 signed a second contract with the team that drafted them. Interestingly, three of us had all 13 drafted, and the worst was still 11. That makes enough sense when you consider we all consistenly have the same ~25 players taken in the first round every year. Also, 10 of the 13 players re-signed were drafted in the top half of the first round.

Most of the 2022 draft class hasn't had to sign an extension yet, so we judged them whether or not any team picked up their fifth year option. 20 of 32 players had their options picked up. For similar reasons, we all had between 16 and 18 of the correct picks.
